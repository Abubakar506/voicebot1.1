FAQ and Procedure Information
1.	Common Patient Questions:
Q: "How often should I come in for a check-up?"
A: "We recommend coming in every six months for a routine check-up to maintain good oral health."
Q: "What should I do in case of a dental emergency?"
A: "If you experience pain, swelling, or bleeding, please contact our clinic immediately or come in for an emergency appointment."
Q: "How long does teeth whitening take?"
A: "In-office teeth whitening typically takes about 1-1.5 hours, depending on the level of whitening desired."
2.	Standard Procedures and Instructions:
Teeth Cleaning: A routine cleaning takes about 45 minutes and includes plaque removal, polishing, and flossing.
Instructions: Avoid eating or drinking for 30 minutes after the cleaning for best results.
Root Canal Treatment: Typically a 1-2 hour procedure that removes infected tissue within a tooth.
Instructions: You may experience soreness afterward; avoid chewing with the affected tooth for a few days.
Teeth Whitening: Uses a peroxide-based gel applied under a UV light to whiten teeth.
Instructions: Avoid staining foods and beverages (like coffee or wine) for 24 hours post-treatment.

Initial Trial Screening
1.	Eligibility Criteria:
Age Requirement: Patients must be at least 18 years old for teeth whitening or dental implants.
Medical History: No current infections or bleeding disorders; patients with a history of severe allergies need medical clearance.
Dental Health Status: Patients must not have untreated cavities for cosmetic procedures.
2.	Screening Responses and Decision Criteria:
Example Screening Question: "Have you had a dental check-up in the past six months?"
Typical Response: Yes or No
Decision: Patients who haven't had a recent check-up may be advised to schedule one first.
Example Screening Question: "Do you have any allergies to anesthesia?"
Typical Response: No
Decision: Patients without anesthesia allergies are cleared for most dental procedures.
